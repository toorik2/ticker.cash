A decentralized BCH/USD price feed on Bitcoin Cash chipnet. Seven notaries co-witness prices from thirteen operator-diverse exchanges; any publisher relays them to a covenant that commits the per-cycle median on chain. No admin keys; the covenant is the only on-chain rule.

§01Trust model

The 7-key notary federation is the only off-chain trust assumption. Each notary independently fetches the price from its pinned CEX over TLS and signs (serverName, sourceId, price, ts, cycleSeq) with its Schnorr key.

Publishers are interchangeable relays. They can't fake notary sigs, alter prices, or skip quorum. A single publisher's only adversarial power is to pick which subset of slot inputs goes into their Oracle.update — but the covenant position-checks the median across ≥ 7 distinct publishers, absorbing ≤ 6 outliers without bias.

Sources are operator-diverse. 9 USD spot markets (Kraken, Coinbase, Gemini, Binance.US, Bitstamp, Crypto.com, Bitfinex, EXMO, Independent Reserve — 4 US, 5 non-US), 2 USDC (OKX, KuCoin), 2 USDT (Bybit, HTX). No operator family appears twice; no quote-currency dominates.

Federation collusion model

What a colluding ≥ 4 of 7 notaries can do:

  • Sign attestations for arbitrary prices the publishers will propagate.

What no collusion can do:

  • Skip the ≥ 7 distinct-publisher quorum check.
  • Replay a stale notary signature — slot's cycleSeq is strict-monotonic.
  • Hide a cycle from observers — every transition is an on-chain tx.
  • Mint new slots after genesis — slot category closed by consensus.
  • Run the chain faster than the 30 s minimum stride enforced by Oracle.cash.

No admin keys. No upgrade path. Adding an 8th notary is a hard cutover: fresh PublisherSlot covenant, new genesis, slot fleet migration.

§02Cycle flow

  1. Publisher polls the Oracle UTXO for prevTs and cycleSeq.
  2. Publisher requests a Schnorr signature from one of the 7 notaries.
  3. Publisher broadcasts PublisherSlot.attest. Slot covenant verifies both Schnorr sigs, the CN/SAN hash, and the monotonic cycleSeq advance; rewrites the slot commit in place.
  4. Publisher waits ~25 s for peer slot refreshes.
  5. Once ≥ 7 slots carry the current cycleSeq, any publisher builds Oracle.update consuming the Oracle + ≥ 7 slots, re-emits each slot unchanged, and mints 2 mutable Tickers.
  6. First to broadcast wins; losers retry on the new tip.

What no signature in this system grants. No signer can change the covenant rules, roll back a confirmed cycle, override the median, or mint new slots after genesis. Every signature is a witness inside a fixed game.

§04Integrate

Two paths.

On-chain (atomic)

Spend a Ticker NFT as a tx input. The Ticker is mutable, anyone-can-spend, self-replicates via the Ticker covenant. Your tx becomes a chain-descendant of the Oracle.update that minted it — BCH consensus refuses to confirm it in any chain history that excludes the update.

input[0]   your consumer covenant
input[1]   Ticker NFT  (from the pinned Oracle category)
output[0]  business logic
output[1]  Ticker re-emit  (Ticker.cash enforces structural equality)
output[2]  optional change

Decode tx.inputs[1].nftCommitment as:

0x80 | seq(4) | lastTs(4) | medianPrice(8)

Little-endian throughout. medianPrice / 10⁸ = USD. Pin the Oracle category (LE-reversed) so an attacker can't feed you a Ticker from a different oracle.

Reference covenant: contracts/examples/PriceGatedRelease.cash — locks BCH, releases on price ≥ strike. ~64 B script body.

Barrier semantics caveat. The reference example accepts any past Ticker that ever cleared the strike — once the gate has been historically reachable, it stays open. For "current price ≥ strike," the protocol needs a freshness witness (planned v12.2).

Off-chain (JSON)

$ curl -s https://usd.ticker.cash/api/v1/price

No auth, CORS open. Schema below.

§05Operate

Two daemons, both stateless. The unified binary scripts/ticker-node.ts wraps either or both.

$ ticker-node --notary --slot 0                # notary only
$ ticker-node --publisher --slot 0 \           # publisher only
    --notary-url https://notary-0.ticker.cash \
    ...×6 more...
$ ticker-node --notary --publisher --slot 0    # bundled

Notary: HTTP daemon holding one Schnorr key. POST /sign returns a signature over the canonical message. Publisher: polls Oracle, requests notary sigs, runs slot.attest() each cycle, races to broadcast Oracle.update. ~12 k sats per cycle.

Source. github.com/toorik2/ticker.cash.
